URLNavigating the relationship between social media use and mental health in the digital age
Author(s)Daniel Opoku, Christian Donkor, John Nana Osei Yeboah, and Linda Quagraine
AbstractIn this peer reviewed journal the authors examine how online platforms like social media impact mental health in people. To collect data and examine this the authors conducted an online survey which consisted of 343 participants. In this study various factors were analyzed including the directionality of comparison with social media users, the frequency and intensity of social comparison, individual differences and vulnerability between social media users, the nature of the content the social media users are exposed to, the salience of comparison between social media users, and comparison targets. All these factors aided to the final findings that most of these finds showed significant influence on mental health outcomes except comparison targets and individual differences.

URLTeenage Cyberbullying Statistics 2025
Author(s)Dr. Shantel Sullivan, Ed.D., LCSW
AbstractIn this webpage written by Dr. Shantel Sullivan on the Bright Path Behavioral Health website she discusses the statistics of teenage cyberbullying in 2025. In the short report Dr. Sullivan acknowledges that cyberbullying follows individuals 24/7 unlike other types of bullying. In the report various factors are analyzed like the prevalence of cyberbullying among teenagers in the US. She also analyzes and details statistics of other factors like gender, ethnicity, and LGBTQ+ teenagers. In the end of her report she answers various questions about topics that cyberbullying affects the risk of developing sleep disorders and at what age and location do teenagers most commonly experience cyberbullying.
